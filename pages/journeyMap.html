Reflecting Freshman to Senior Year

When I first started college as a freshman, I was excited but also unsure of what direction my journey would take. I knew I was interested in design and technology, but I wasn't fully confident in my skills yet or how I would fit into the professional world. One of my biggest goals early on was to push myself outside my comfort zone, get involved on campus, and gain hands-on experience that would help me grow both creatively and professionally. I quickly learned that college would be less about having everything figured out and more about being open to learning and trying new things.

Looking back now, I'm proud of how much I've grown since those early semesters. Throughout my time at IU Indianapolis, I've taken on a variety of roles that shaped my college experience. I became involved in leadership and mentorship positions, including serving as a co-leader for OTEAM and as a Teaching Assistant for an Introduction to Web Design course. I also balanced creative and professional roles outside the classroom, working as a brand and web designer, content strategist, and bobarista, all while continuing to build my portfolio and refine my skills in web design, development, and graphic design.

These experiences allowed me to build strong connections with classmates, faculty, and professionals while developing skills such as communication, leadership, time management, and problem-solving. Some of the most meaningful moments came from collaborating on real-world projects, mentoring younger students, and working on design work that addressed social issues and community impact. Through internships and hands-on projects, I gained valuable industry experience that helped bridge the gap between academic learning and professional practice.

As I look ahead, I feel confident and prepared for the next chapter of my journey. Everything I've been involved in—from coursework to leadership roles to internships—has helped shape who I am as a designer and professional. While my goals continue to evolve, I'm excited to carry the lessons, skills, and connections I've gained into my future career, knowing that my college experience has laid a strong foundation for what's next.
