Design for Dignity

Design for Dignity began as a collaborative class project between Indiana University Indianapolis, Yonsei University in South Korea, and the Boner Community Center. The project focused on elderly health and well-being, exploring a range of topics including hydration, exercise and nutrition synergy, chronic condition meal planning, and overall quality of life for aging populations.

The goal of the collaboration was to use design as a tool to communicate accessible, research-based health information to support older adults in both local and global communities. Students worked across cultures to research elderly health challenges and develop visual materials — including brochures, posters, and educational resources — that addressed real-world needs.

What started as a classroom collaboration evolved into something more. Through my honors contract, I expanded the project by developing a fully functional website for Design for Dignity. The website serves as a digital platform to showcase the collaborative work, communicate the initiative’s mission, and make these resources more accessible to a broader audience.

As part of this honors experience, I worked on both the frontend development and portions of backend implementation while collaborating with a senior design student and design professional Amritta Data to bring the digital experience to life.

My Role & Development Process

Through my honors contract, I was responsible for transforming the collaborative design work into a fully responsive and interactive website. While the original project focused on printed materials, my role required rethinking how those materials would function in a digital environment.

I developed the frontend structure of the site, translating visual mockups into responsive layouts using clean, organized code. This included building reusable components, establishing consistent visual hierarchy, and ensuring the website maintained accessibility standards across devices.

In addition to frontend development, I contributed to portions of the backend implementation to support content organization and functionality. This required careful testing, debugging, and refinement to ensure a seamless user experience.

A key focus of the website was intentional motion design. Rather than using animation purely for visual interest, I implemented subtle transitions and interactive elements to guide users through content, emphasize important information, and reinforce the project's themes of dignity, care, and empowerment.

Design Approach & Digital Experience

The Design for Dignity website was created to extend the impact of the original classroom materials into a cohesive and accessible digital experience. Rather than simply uploading brochures and posters online, the goal was to design a site that felt intentional, structured, and emotionally aligned with the initiative’s mission.

Visually, the site emphasizes clarity and readability to ensure accessibility for a wide range of users. Clean layouts, balanced spacing, and strong typographic hierarchy guide visitors through content without overwhelming them. Because the subject matter centers around elderly health and dignity, the design avoids clutter and instead prioritizes calm, approachable visuals.

Motion design plays a subtle but meaningful role throughout the site. Scroll-triggered animations and smooth transitions are used to create pacing and guide user attention. These interactions are intentionally restrained, reinforcing the themes of care and respect rather than distracting from the content itself.

The website is fully responsive, adapting seamlessly across desktop, tablet, and mobile devices. This responsiveness was critical in ensuring that the resources are accessible to a broader audience, including caregivers, community members, and partner organizations.

Ultimately, the digital platform transforms Design for Dignity from a classroom collaboration into a lasting, accessible initiative that can continue to grow beyond the semester.

Reflection

The Design for Dignity honors contract became much more than an extension of a class project. It challenged me to think beyond assignments and approach design as a long-term, impactful solution. Transforming a collaborative academic initiative into a fully developed digital platform required both technical growth and a deeper understanding of responsibility as a designer.

Through this experience, I strengthened my frontend development skills and gained confidence contributing to backend functionality, but more importantly, I learned how to design with intention. Every layout decision, animation choice, and structural element had to support clarity, accessibility, and dignity. This project reinforced that thoughtful design is not about decoration — it is about communication, inclusion, and impact.

Working across cultures with Yonsei University and developing the website in collaboration with a design professional pushed me to operate at a higher level of professionalism. I learned how to navigate feedback, iterate thoughtfully, and balance creative vision with technical execution. These experiences mirrored real industry environments and strengthened my readiness for professional web development work.

Most significantly, Design for Dignity solidified my belief that digital platforms can extend the life and reach of meaningful initiatives. By transforming research and printed materials into an accessible online experience, I saw how web development can serve communities and amplify important conversations around health and well-being.

This honors contract not only advanced my technical abilities, but also deepened my commitment to using design and development as tools for advocacy, accessibility, and social impact.
