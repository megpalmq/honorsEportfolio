IU Student Affairs

Since March 2025, I have worked at IUI's Division of Undergraduate Education (Student Affairs) as a web intern, and it has been one of the most valuable experiences of my college career.

I have gained real-world experience in software development and website management while learning how to communicate with clients, adapt quickly, and contribute to high-impact campus projects.

Cascade CMS (WCMS)

Cascade CMS is where much of our day-to-day web work happens. Working in WCMS taught me how modern web teams manage large sites without rebuilding everything from scratch.

  • Navigated and maintained large page structures.
  • Managed updates and deployments with quality control.
  • Helped train others on CMS workflows.

ServiceNow

ServiceNow is a ticketing system that the Comm. team uses. We get sent in tickets from various faculty members and students requesting usually to edit webpages, create webpages, request access to certain tools, and general things like that. We claim tickets in a chat system called Slack, which is very similar to discord, and we complete the tickets. Usually the tickets we receive are for very small changes that only take between 5-15 minutes to complete. After we have completed what the ticket asks, we turn it in. Pretty simple.

  • Created and maintained internal documentation.
  • Built and delivered onboarding/training workflows.
  • Strengthened client communication through ticketing process.

Salesforce Marketing Cloud

Salesforce Marketing Cloud gave me hands-on experience designing and deploying large-scale email communications. It also helped me better understand subscriber data, preferences, and accessibility.

  • Designed and deployed targeted email campaigns.
  • Managed subscriber lists and communication preferences.
  • Applied accessibility best practices to email design.

Leadership and Project Ownership

Through the IUI On-Campus Internship Program, I set a goal to lead projects and mentor newer interns. I learned how to assign work based on strengths, protect team bandwidth, and keep timelines moving.

That leadership experience taught me that good project management is not about control. It is about communication, accountability, and helping the team succeed together.

Reflection

I am incredibly thankful for my time in Student Affairs. This internship has proven that you get out what you put in, and the effort I invested came back as stronger technical skills, leadership confidence, and clarity about my future in software and web development.
